Overview

Kato clients produce pitch and reporting decks in PowerPoint, populated by hand with data that already lives in Kato. We're building a platform that removes that manual step entirely: import your own PowerPoint template, map it to live platform data, and generate bespoke, on-brand decks. Or skip templates and design presentations from scratch, effectively rebuilding PowerPoint and Canva inside Kato.

I'm both the product designer and the front-end developer on this platform: a hybrid role made possible by AI-assisted development built on top of our design system.

The problem

Clients' decks were built manually, outside the platform:

  • Double-keying: data lived in Kato but was re-typed into PowerPoint slide by slide
  • Hours of production time per deck: how long did a typical deck take?
  • Brand consistency depended on discipline: every manually edited deck drifted from the template
  • No scale: many bespoke decks meant repeating the same manual work every time

The result: slow turnaround on client-facing documents, errors from manual data entry, and a key output of the platform's data living entirely outside the platform.

The approach

01Template import & data mapping

Clients bring their own PowerPoint templates; the platform preserves them faithfully and makes them data-aware.

  • Import existing .pptx templates with layouts, fonts, and brand styling intact
  • Map placeholders to live Kato data: what maps? property details, transactions?
  • Generate bespoke decks automatically: same template, different data, zero re-keying

02The editor: rebuilding PowerPoint and Canva

For clients starting from scratch, a full editing experience inside the platform, designed for non-designers, respecting the muscle memory people bring from PowerPoint while removing its sharp edges. On-brand by default: brand assets and styles are built in, so bespoke doesn't mean off-brand.

Key challenge

Users arrive with 20 years of expectations. The bar isn't "good editor"; it's "doesn't break what my hands already know."

03One person, both roles

This is the first Kato product built with a single person spanning design and front-end, enabled by AI.

  • Designed in Figma, shipped in production code: no handoff, no translation loss
  • AI-assisted development (Claude, Cursor) with the design system as shared context: tokens, components, and usage rules keeping generated code consistent with design
  • Tight loop: decisions tested in the real product within hours, not sprints
Why it matters

The design/build split is where product speed usually dies. Collapsing it changed not just velocity but the design itself. I design differently when I know exactly what the front-end can do.

Learnings
  • When one person designs and builds, the bottleneck shifts from handoff to decision-making; AI removes the skill barrier, not the judgement
  • Rebuilding familiar tools is a constraint, not a blank canvas; users' PowerPoint muscle memory is part of the spec
  • A design system is what makes AI-generated UI trustworthy; consistency has to come from somewhere
  • Building the front-end made me a better designer: feasibility stopped being a guess
